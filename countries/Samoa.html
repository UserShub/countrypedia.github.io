Samoa

Samoa, officially the Independent State of Samoa and until 1997 known as Western Samoa, is a Polynesian island country consisting of two main islands, two smaller, inhabited islands, and several smaller, uninhabited islands, including the Aleipata Islands.


Capital : Apia


Majority Languages spoken : English


Currency: Samoan tālā


Area: 2,830 km²


Major Cities : Apia, Upolu, Petit Savai'i


Geography : The Sāmoan archipelago is a chain of 16 islands and numerous seamounts covering 3,123 km² in the central South Pacific, south of the equator, about halfway between Hawaii and New Zealand, forming part of Polynesia and of the wider region of Oceania.
