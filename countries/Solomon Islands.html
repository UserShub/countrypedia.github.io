Solomon Islands

The Solomon Islands, a nation of hundreds of islands in the South Pacific, has many WWII-era sites. Guadalcanal, a province and one of the archipelago’s largest islands, honors fallen Allied soldiers at its U.S. War Memorial. Guadalcanal is also home to the nation's capital, Honiara, whose bustling Central Market showcases the islands' produce and traditional handicrafts.


Capital : Honiara


Majority Languages spoken : English


Currency: Solomon Islands dollar


Area: 27,990 km²


Major Cities : Honiara, Buala, Auki


Geography : Solomon Islands are 2 parallel chains of volcanic islands and small coral atolls in the south-western Pacific. The country is mainly mountainous and covered in forests, although it has some extensive plains. Solomon Islands' nearest neighbours are Vanuatu to the south-east and Papua New Guinea to the west.
