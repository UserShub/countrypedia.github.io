ICELAND

Iceland, a Nordic island nation, is defined by its dramatic landscape with volcanoes, geysers, hot springs and lava fields. Massive glaciers are protected in Vatnajökull and Snæfellsjökull national parks. Most of the population lives in the capital, Reykjavik, which runs on geothermal power and is home to the National and Saga museums, tracing Iceland's Viking history.


Capital : Reykjavík


Majority Languages spoken : Icelandic


Currency: Icelandic króna


Area: 100,250 km²


Major Cities : Vik, Reykjavík, Akureyri


Geography : Iceland is an island country at the confluence of the North Atlantic and Arctic oceans, east of Greenland and immediately south of the Arctic Circle, atop the constructive boundary of the northern Mid-Atlantic Ridge about 860 km from Scotland and 4,200 km from New York City.
